Cookies & LocalStorge

Cookies and localStorage are both web storage technologies used to store data on the user's browser.

Cookies are small text files that are created by websites and stored on the user's computer.

localStorage, on the other hand, is a client-side storage technology that allows web applications to store data on the user's browser.

Both cookies and localStorage are widely used by web developers to provide a better user experience and to store information that can be used to personalize content and services. However, it is important to note that cookies can be used for tracking and other privacy-invasive purposes, while localStorage can potentially be accessed by malicious actors if the website is compromised.

Content Blockers & Add Blockers

Content blockers and ad blockers are software tools that can be used to block unwanted content from being displayed or downloaded on a device.

Ad blockers typically block ads displayed on websites, such as banner ads, pop-up ads, and video ads. Ad blockers also prevent tracking scripts from following a user's online activity, which helps protect their privacy.

Content blockers, on the other hand, can block a wider range of content, such as images, videos, audio, scripts, and even entire websites. Content blockers can be used to block adult content, gambling websites, social media, and other types of content that may be deemed inappropriate or distracting.

Overall, ad blockers and content blockers provide a more streamlined and private browsing experience for users by blocking unwanted content and improving page load times.
